What is this?

Most multi-robot coordination research assumes agents can communicate, know their own capabilities, or at least understand something about the tasks they are solving. This project removes all three.

Zero-Knowledge Multi-Robot Task Allocation (ZK-MRTA) is a problem setting where a swarm of drones must allocate themselves to targets under the strictest possible informational constraints:

No knowledge of task properties No self-knowledge of capabilities No knowledge of other agents No direct communication Outcome-based learning only

All an agent ever sees is a public broadcast of what the swarm did last step and what reward each drone received. From that alone, the swarm must learn to coordinate.

The Core Idea

Each drone independently runs a matrix factorization model — the same idea behind collaborative filtering in recommendation systems, repurposed for online latent-utility estimation. Drones are users, targets are items, engagement outcomes are ratings.

Because the public broadcast exposes every drone's action and reward, each drone updates its local model not just from its own shots, but from the entire observed swarm interaction. No parameters are shared. No messages are passed. Collaboration emerges through the shared observation stream alone. 

Hidden:      z_i · z_j = compatibility  (never observed)
Reward:      r_ij = cosine(z_i, z_j)   (noisy, public)

Each drone learns:  P[i] · U[j] ≈ r_ij   via online SGD
Action:      argmax_j  P[self] · U[j]   over active targets

Results

Single seed, 35 training episodes, 9 drones, 27 targets, latent dim = 3. The MF policy closes ~92% of the efficiency gap to a fully-privileged oracle.

Experimental Roadmap

The current results are preliminary (single seed). Planned evaluation:

  1. Multi-seed paired benchmark — 20 seeds, all three policies, reproducibility assessment
  2. Supervision mode ablation — direct reward vs. integration-matrix smoothing
  3. Latent-dimension sensitivity — factorization rank vs. hidden environment rank
  4. Noise sensitivity — separate sweeps for observation noise and reward noise
  5. Swarm-scale scaling — 3:9, 6:18, 9:27, 15:45 drone-target configurations
